How it works

Three steps to a rig-proof record

No training required. If you can take a photo, you can defend the vote at your polling unit.

1

Witness the count

Stay for the public counting after voting closes. Photograph the result sheet (EC8A) the moment it's announced.

2

Sign & submit

Your report is cryptographically signed on your own phone with GPS and time — proof it came from that place, at that moment.

3

Corroborate

Counts only become trusted when independent observers at the same unit agree. One person can't fake it — that's the point.

Why trust it

Built so nobody — including us — can quietly change a number

Every report is chained to the one before it on a public ledger. Change anything and the chain breaks visibly. Anyone can verify it — no permission needed. Verify the ledger yourself →

  • Tamper-evident hash-chain ledger, publicly auditable
  • Reports signed on the observer's device — keys never leave the phone
  • Independent corroboration required before a count is trusted
  • Phone numbers stored only as one-way hashes
  • Open evidence: photos, GPS and totals visible to everyone
