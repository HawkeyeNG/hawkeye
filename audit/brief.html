Hawkeye lets ordinary Nigerians photograph, cryptographically sign, and permanently publish the result announced at their polling unit — creating an independent, tamper-evident public record before those results can be altered on the way to collation. Open source (MIT), mobile-first, and built so that no single actor — not the state, not a party, not even the Hawkeye team — can quietly rewrite a result.

The problem

Nigerian elections are most vulnerable in the gap between the polling unit — where votes are counted publicly — and collation, where result sheets have historically been altered, swapped, or lost with no independent record to compare against. In 2023 the failure of INEC's official results portal (IReV) on election night collapsed public trust in the official channel and fueled disinformation on every side. There is no widely trusted, citizen-driven, independently verifiable record of the unit-level count. Hawkeye creates one.

How it works — verify, don't trust

  • Signed on the citizen's own device. Each report is signed with an ECDSA-P256 key that never leaves the phone — the server cannot forge one.
  • Hash-chain ledger. Every report commits to all before it; anyone recomputes the whole chain in their own browser.
  • External anchoring. Ledger heads are published daily to Sigstore's public Rekor transparency log, which we don't control — a database rollback is detectable by anyone.
  • Per-race Merkle proofs. Any one of ~1,500 contests is verifiable in isolation for a tribunal, without replaying the rest.
  • Corroboration + AI screening. Counts trusted only on independent agreement; advisory AI flags forgeries, provenance and anomalies — never decides.
  • Crowd arbitration. Flagged results are re-judged publicly by verified observers under a published quorum rule — on their own anchored chain.

Live public-audit page: anyone recomputes the entire hash chain in-browser and checks each ledger head against Sigstore Rekor — no trust in Hawkeye required.
