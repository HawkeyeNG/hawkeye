Observer Guide

Everything you need to do — before, during and after the count at your polling unit.

Before election day

1 Verify your phone

On the report page, enter your Nigerian mobile number and confirm the one-time code sent to you on Telegram. Do this days ahead — not in the queue.

2 Map your polling unit

Visit your unit, open Map a Polling Unit, and capture its GPS position while standing there. Once a few people do this, the unit is geofenced for election day — reports for it can then only come from people actually present.

3 Do a dry run

Open the report page once on Wi-Fi so the app is cached to your phone, grant camera and location permission, and make sure your battery plan for the day includes the evening count.

On election day

4 Stay for the count

Voting ends, then the presiding officer counts ballots publicly and fills in the EC8A result sheet for each election. This — the announcement — is the moment that matters.

5 Photograph the EC8A sheet
  • Point the camera at the whole sheet — the scanner outlines it and captures automatically when steady.
  • Good light, no glare, no fingers over the figures column.
  • If the app warns the photo is blurry or washed out, retake it.
6 Photograph the venue

A separate photo of the polling unit itself — the building, banner or surroundings. This lets the system confirm different observers stood at the same place.

7 Enter the counts and submit immediately

Type each party's announced figure exactly, then sign and submit while still at the unit. Photos expire if you wait, and your GPS must match where the photos were taken. Several elections may be counted at your unit the same day — submit one report per election, each with its own photos.

The rules that keep reports honest

Hard rules — the app enforces these
  • Live photos only; no gallery uploads, no screenshots.
  • One report per election per polling unit per observer — and one per election per device, regardless of how many SIMs it holds.
  • Reports are permanent. Nothing can be edited or deleted after signing.
  • Geofenced units only accept reports from people standing there.

If something goes wrong

Troubleshooting
  • GPS too weak — step into open sky, away from roofing, and retry.
  • No Telegram code — reopen the bot link and share your contact; the number must match the one you typed.
  • "Photos too old" — retake both photos and submit straight away.
  • Session expired — verify your phone again; your identity and history are preserved.
