Privacy & Data

Hawkeye is built to protect the people who use it. This explains exactly what we collect, why, what becomes public, and what stays private.

What we collect
  • Your phone number — stored only as a one-way cryptographic hash. We never keep or display the actual number; it exists so one number equals one observer identity.
  • Your reports — the counts you enter and two live photos (the result sheet and the venue), each stamped with the GPS location where it was taken.
  • A device fingerprint — a hash of your device's characteristics, to stop one phone from filing multiple reports for the same race.
  • Your Telegram link — if you verify or subscribe via Telegram, we store the chat ID so the bot can send your code and alerts. We never read your Telegram messages or contacts.
What becomes public

To be a credible transparency tool, the evidence must be open. Your submitted counts, photos and their locations are published on a public, tamper-evident ledger and cannot be edited or deleted — that permanence is what makes them trustworthy. Your phone number is never part of this; reports are tied to an anonymous observer ID, not to you personally. Incident media is shown publicly only after human review.

What stays private
  • Your actual phone number (only the hash is stored).
  • Your identity — reports are not publicly linked to your name or number.
  • One-time codes, which expire within minutes and are deleted once used.
What we never do
  • We do not sell, rent or share your data with anyone.
  • We run no advertising and no third-party trackers.
  • We use no cookies — the app keeps only a sign-in token and keys in your browser's local storage, on your device.
Security

All traffic is encrypted (HTTPS). Phone numbers are HMAC-hashed, reports are signed on your own device with a key that never leaves it, and access is rate-limited and bot-screened.

Your rights

You can delete your observer ID yourself at any time: open the menu while signed in and choose Delete my ID. This removes your signing key, device link, Telegram link and alert subscriptions. Two things remain, by design: public ledger entries (permanent and anonymous — that permanence is what makes them trustworthy) and the one-way hash of your number, kept so a deleted identity can't be reused to file duplicate reports. Re-registering the same number restores your original observer ID. Other Nigeria Data Protection Act requests: info@hawkeye.com.ng.

Hawkeye is an independent transparency initiative and is not affiliated with INEC. Official results are those declared by INEC.
