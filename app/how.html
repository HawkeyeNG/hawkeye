How Hawkeye Works

Hawkeye turns thousands of ordinary phones into a network of independent witnesses. No single report is trusted on its own — trust is earned when separate people, provably at the same place, say the same thing.

How a report becomes trusted

  1. Photograph the evidence. Two live photos — the EC8A sheet (auto-scanned and flattened) and the venue — each GPS-stamped the moment it is taken. Gallery uploads are impossible.
  2. Sign on your device. The counts, photo fingerprints and location are signed with a key that never leaves your phone, then chained onto a public ledger. Nothing can be edited or quietly deleted afterwards.
  3. Independent agreement. A count is only marked verified when separate observers at the same unit report matching numbers — corroborated by GPS clustering, venue-photo matching and OCR of the sheet itself.

The machinery underneath

🪪 One person, one identity

  • A verified phone number is one observer identity. Numbers are stored only as one-way hashes — never readable, never published.
  • Every phone also carries a device fingerprint. One device can report each race once, no matter how many SIM cards or accounts it holds.

📍 Location that proves itself

  • Units with a confirmed location are geofenced: reports are only accepted from people standing there.
  • Units without one accept reports, but the count stays visibly "location-unverified" until at least three independent observers' GPS positions cluster at the same spot.
  • Before election day, volunteers crowd-map units the same way — three agreeing fixes confirm a unit's location in advance.
  • Every unit also has an approximate area from open geographic data; reports or mapping fixes far outside it are rejected outright.

📸 Evidence that can't be recycled

  • Photos are captured live in the app — the EC8A sheet is auto-detected, flattened and quality-checked (blur/glare) like a document scanner.
  • Each photo is GPS-stamped at capture; the sheet, venue and submission positions must all agree, and photos expire within minutes.
  • Re-submitting someone else's photo — or a near-identical copy — is detected and rejected by image fingerprinting.
  • Venue photos from different observers are feature-matched to confirm they stood at the same physical place.
  • The typed counts are cross-checked against the digits machine-read from the sheet photograph itself.

⛓️ A ledger nobody can quietly edit

  • Each report is signed on the observer's phone with a key that never leaves it, then chained to the previous entry by cryptographic hash.
  • Changing or removing any past entry breaks the chain publicly. Anyone can re-verify it in their browser on the Verify the Ledger page.

📊 From reports to the leaderboard

  • Matching counts from independent observers push a unit's confidence up; conflicting counts mark it disputed — visibly, never silently resolved.
  • Each report is tagged to its races automatically — state, senatorial district and federal constituency come from the official register.
  • The national leaderboard rolls verified units up per race, with grey regions still awaiting reports.

🔔 Alerts

Follow any race — everywhere, or a single state, district or constituency — and Hawkeye pings you on Telegram whenever new reports land. Subscribe on the results page.

What Hawkeye is not: Hawkeye does not declare winners and its tallies are not official results. It is a public, independent evidence record of what was announced at polling units. Official results are those declared by INEC.
