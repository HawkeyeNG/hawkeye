# Evidentiary Form

A design movement for institutions that must be believed by people who have
every reason not to believe. Evidentiary Form holds that trust is not decorated
into existence — it is constructed, the way a proof is constructed: from few
axioms, in plain sight, with every joint load-bearing and every line accountable.
The work must look like it could be entered into a court record. Its beauty is
the beauty of an instrument that has been calibrated a thousand times by hands
that refused to stop at nine hundred and ninety-nine.

Space is testimony. The composition breathes the way a hearing room breathes —
formal, symmetrical, unhurried, with generous silence around the thing being
examined. Negative space is not emptiness; it is the absence of anything to
hide. Forms sit on a strict modular grid, centered with ceremonial gravity, and
every dimension resolves to the module — nothing eyeballed, nothing almost.
The eye should sense, without measuring, that measuring would only confirm.

Color is sworn to restraint. One deep ink, near-black but warmed toward green
like a forest at dusk; one civic green, saturated and incorruptible; one paper
tone, the off-white of archival stock that has waited decades to be read. No
gradient pleads, no shadow flatters. Where the green appears it appears as
evidence appears — sparingly, precisely, at the exact point of consequence.

Form is spliced, never collaged. When two objects must become one mark, they
fuse at the level of geometry — shared axes, shared radii, shared silhouette —
so the hybrid reads as a single inevitable body, as if the two were always one
tool waiting to be noticed. Circles are compass-true, corners are radiused by
rule, and the mark must survive being one centimeter tall, one color deep,
carved in a courthouse lintel or inked on a thumb-smudged receipt.

Typography testifies briefly and under oath. A high-contrast serif speaks the
name — once, with the finality of a gavel. A hairline monospace annotates like
a registrar: figure numbers, dimensions, dates, nothing more. Text never
explains the image; it files the image. Around the central form, faint
construction lines and dimension ticks may remain visible, the way a master
draughtsman leaves the pencil under the ink — proof of process, painstaking
attention made quietly visible.

The finished artifact must feel labored over to the edge of obsession —
master-level execution in which alignment, optical correction, and spacing have
been revised past the point anyone would notice, because the ones it must
convince are exactly the ones who look that closely. It should hang equally
well in a museum of modern design and in the marble hallway of a tribunal, and
in both rooms it should say the same thing: this was made carefully, because it
matters.
