Frequently Asked Questions

Is Hawkeye official? Are these the real election results?

No. Hawkeye is an independent, community-run transparency initiative. The tallies shown here are crowd-verified evidence of what was announced at individual polling units — the official results of any election are only those declared by INEC.

Who can be an observer?

Anyone with a Nigerian mobile number and a phone with a camera and GPS. Verify your number once, then report from the polling unit where you observe the count. No accreditation, membership or payment is involved.

What happens to my phone number?

It is hashed (one-way scrambled) before storage and is never published or shared. It exists solely so that one number equals one observer identity.

Why does verification use Telegram?

Telegram delivers your one-time code and later your race alerts free of charge. Open the bot link shown at sign-up, share your contact (it must match the number you typed), and the code arrives instantly.

Why must photos be taken live? Can I upload from my gallery?

No gallery uploads, ever. Both photos — the EC8A results sheet and the venue — are captured in-app and GPS-stamped at the moment of capture. This is what makes a Hawkeye report hard to fake from somewhere else.

Can a report be edited or deleted afterwards?

No. Every report is signed on the observer's device and chained onto a public, tamper-evident ledger. Anyone can re-verify the whole chain on the Verify the Ledger page. If a report is wrong, the remedy is more independent reports — matching counts from separate observers outweigh a bad one.

When is a count marked "verified"?

When independent observers at the same unit submit matching counts, corroborated by GPS clustering, venue-photo matching and machine-reading of the sheet itself. Until then a count stays visibly marked as reported or provisional.

My polling unit doesn't appear near me. Why?

Most of Nigeria's 176,846 polling units have no published GPS location. You can still report — find your unit through the register browser (state → LGA → ward). Better still, map your unit before election day so it is geofenced on the day.

Can one person submit multiple reports with different SIM cards?

Each device carries a fingerprint, and one device can report each race only once — no matter how many numbers are registered on it. Independent reports must come from genuinely different people on different phones.

How do I get alerts for races I care about?

Verify your phone once, then on the results page choose an election and a state, district or constituency and press Follow. You'll get a Telegram message whenever new reports land.
